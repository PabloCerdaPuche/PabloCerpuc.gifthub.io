Easy Peppermint Fudge

  • Time: 2 hrs 10 min
  • Yields: 10-12 pieces
Ingredients Quantity
cooking spray squirt
semisweet chocolate chips 3 cups
sweetened condensed milk 1 can, 14-ounce
red & white peppermint candies, crushed up to you

Step 1: Line an 8-inch square baking pan with foil. Spray the foil with cooking spray.

Step 2: In a medium saucepan over low heat, combine the chocolate chips and sweetened condensed milk and stir until it's all melted and smooth. Pour it into the prepared pan and spread to even out the surface. Sprinkle on the crushed candies. Refrigerate for 2 hours.

Step 3: Lift the foil out of the pan and peel it off the fudge. Cut into small squares.

Molasses Cookies

  • Time: 20 min
  • Yields: 36 cookies
Ingredients Quantity
all-purpose flour 2-1/2 cups
baking soda 2 tsp
ground cinnamon 1 tsp
ground ginger 1 tsp
ground cloves 1/2 tsp
kosher salt pinch
unsalted butter, room temp 1-1/2 sticks
brown sugar 1 cup
egg 1 large
molasses 3/4 cup
turbinado sugar for coating

Step 1: Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F.

Step 2: In a medium bowl, combine the flour, baking soda, cinnamon, ginger, cloves and salt and set aside.

Step 3: In the bowl of a standing mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, cream together the butter and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in the egg and then the molasses. In thirds, gently mix in the flour mixture until just combined.

Step 4: Using an ice cream scoop, scoop out the cookie dough into 1-inch balls onto a sheet tray covered with turbinado sugar. Gently toss the dough balls in the sugar, covering them completely. Place the sugar-coated dough balls on another sheet tray and gently squish a little.

Step 5: Bake for 9 to 10 minutes. Remove the cookies to a rack to cool. Store in an airtight container for up to 2 weeks.

Old Fashioned Sugar Cookies

  • Time: 4 hr 40 min
  • Yields: 3 dozen cookies, depending on cookie cutter size
Ingredients Quantity
all-purpose flour 3 cups
unsalted butter 2 sticks
granulated sugar 1 cup
eggs, lightly beaten 2
vanilla 1 tsp
baking powder 2 tsp
salt 1 tsp
Icing Ingredients
confectioners' sugar 2 pounds
meringue powder (egg white powder) 1/2 cup + 2 tbsp
food coloring as needed

Step 1: Cream the butter and sugar in a large bowl with an electric mixer. Beat in the eggs and vanilla.

Step 2: Combine the flour, baking powder and salt in a second large bowl and mix well. Stir the flour mixture into the butter mixture 1 cup at a time. Chill the dough for 3 to 4 hours.

Step 3: Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F. Line a cookie sheet with parchment. Roll out the dough and cut it into shapes with cookie cutters or a knife. Place the shapes on the prepared cookie sheet. If decorating with colored sugar, brush the cookies with milk and sprinkle with colored sugar (if using royal icing, leave unfinished). Bake until the cookies are just beginning to brown around the edges, 10 to 15 minutes, depending on the size of the cookies.

Step 4: Remove the cookies to a rack to cool completely. If using royal icing, decorate the cookies as desired.

Royal Icing:

Step 1: Combine the confectioners' sugar, meringue powder and 3/4 cup water in a large bowl. Mix slowly with an electric mixer until stiff enough to form peaks; the icing should be pure white and thick, but not fluffy and bubbly. If the frosting is over-beaten, it will get aerated which makes it harder to work with. If this happens, let the frosting sit to settle, and then use a rubber spatula to vigorously beat and smooth out the frosting.

Step 2: Add up to 1 tablespoon food coloring and mix with a rubber spatula until the color is uniform. Gels are best with royal icing. You don't want to thin them with liquid colors. Be careful of adding too much color, which reduces the sheen of the frosting and can break down the consistency of the frosting over a couple of days. Store the icing at room temperature, covered, with plastic wrap on the surface.

Yield: 3-1/2 cups icing
